Saxon Gold

The saxon gold or the Staffordshire hoard as it is more commonly known as was discovered in July 2009 by a metalstector enthusiast in a field in Hammerwich. The hoard consisted of over 3,500 gold and silver artifects which makes it the sole most extravigent anglo saxon treasure find ever throughout the nation. The hoard was on display for a limited time in Lichfield Cathedral where visitors could not only see the find but could also learn about the connection between the Cathedral's other historical treasure such as St Chad's Gospel and The Lichfield Angel. The hoard was also on display at Tamworth Castle between 27th August and 18th September befoure going to the Birmingham museum where it currently resides.
